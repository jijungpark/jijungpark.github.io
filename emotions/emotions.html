Exploring Emotions

Conveying emotions through web drawings

CLICK TO SEE THE WEB DRAWINGS

Objective

To convey the various emotions that we commonly experience in our everyday lives - anger, sadness, and surprise - through web drawings. To explore the power of shapes, lines, colors, and animation techniques in eliciting emotional responses.

Process

1. Understanding Facial Features
First, I analyzed how manipulating different parts of facial features could express different emotions. I only focused on the eyes and the eyebrows because manipulating the mouth would make the emotions too obvious and I wanted my web drawings to express the emotions in a subtle manner. I had to draw various facial expressions on a piece of paper to examine how manipulating the position of the eyes and the eyebrows could display different emotions.
2. Animating SVG Icons
I created original SVG icons for the eyes and the eyebrows using Adobe Illustrator. Then, I animating them using CSS transitions to display the three different facial expressions for anger, sadness, and surprise.
3. Creating Background Effects
After manipulating the SVG icons, I experimented with lines, shapes, and colors in the background to elicit the three different emotions. For the emotion of anger, I used a canvas animation to draw random, bold strokes of line in the background. I made the background color black and applied a drop shadow effect to the SVG icons to create a contrasting effect. For the emotion of sadness, I created a blue linear gradient and animated transparent bubbles in the background to elicit the feeling of melancholy. For the emotion of surprise, I created ellipses of different sizes and colors and made them appear in random positions.

Challenges / Shortcomings

Animating each of the SVG icons was challenging for I had to be very cautious and precise with the amount of degree I was going to rotate and translate. One of the shortcomings for this project is that the emotion of surprise is not conveyed as well as the other two emotions.
